# Organization Background

## Scenario -
A mid-sized IT services company (500–800 employees) operating in India manages its HR processes using:

Excel sheets for employee records
Email threads for leave approvals
Shared folders for onboarding documents

The company is scaling rapidly and facing operational inefficiencies.

## Problem Statement -
As the organization grows, the existing manual HR processes are no longer sustainable:

Key Issues:
* Employee data is scattered across multiple files = data inconsistency
* Leave approvals are handled via email = delays & no visibility
* No real-time leave balance tracking = frequent conflicts
* Onboarding process is manual = missed steps & delays
* No centralized system = lack of reporting & audit trail

## Business Goals -
The organization wants to implement a centralized HRIS system to:
* Digitize and standardize HR processes
* Improve efficiency and reduce manual effort
* Provide real-time visibility into employee data
* Enable structured workflows (leave, onboarding, attendance)
* Improve employee and manager experience
