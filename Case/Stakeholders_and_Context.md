# Stakeholder & Project Context Document

### HRIS Employee Lifecycle Management System 

---

## 1. Project Background

A mid-sized IT services organization is transitioning from manual HR processes (Excel, email-based workflows) to a centralized HRIS platform to improve efficiency, data accuracy, and employee experience.

The project is planned as a **multi-phase implementation** covering onboarding, leave management, employee self-service, and full lifecycle management.

---

## 2. Project Objectives

* Digitize and standardize HR processes
* Reduce manual effort and operational delays
* Improve data accuracy and accessibility
* Enable employee self-service capabilities
* Provide real-time visibility into HR operations
* Enhance overall employee and manager experience

---

## 3. Stakeholder Identification

| Stakeholder | Role            | Responsibilities                                  | Interest Level | Influence |
| ----------- | --------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | -------------- | --------- |
| HR Team     | Primary Users   | Manage employee data, onboarding, policies        | High           | High      |
| Employees   | End Users       | Apply leave, update data, access information      | High           | Medium    |
| Managers    | Approvers       | Approve leave, manage team availability           | High           | High      |
| IT/Admin    | System Owners   | Maintain system, manage access and configurations | Medium         | High      |
| Leadership  | Decision Makers | Monitor reports, approve strategic initiatives    | Medium         | High      |

---

## 4. Stakeholder Analysis

### High Influence – High Interest

* HR Team
* Managers

Require continuous engagement, requirement validation, and feedback loops

---

### High Influence – Low Interest

* Leadership
* IT/Admin

Require periodic updates and milestone reporting

---

### Low Influence – High Interest

* Employees

Require intuitive system design and clear communication

---

## 5. Communication Plan

| Stakeholder | Communication Type             | Frequency | Mode                      |
| ----------- | ------------------------------ | --------- | ------------------------- |
| HR Team     | Requirement Discussions, Demos | Weekly    | Meetings / walkthroughs   |
| Managers    | Workflow Validation            | Bi-weekly | Meetings / Email          |
| Employees   | Updates & Announcements        | As needed | Dashboard / Notifications |
| Leadership  | Progress Updates               | Monthly   | Reports                   |
| IT/Admin    | Technical Coordination         | Weekly    | Meetings                  |

---

## 6. Assumptions

* Users have basic system literacy
* Managers follow defined approval hierarchies
* HR policies are predefined and standardized
* System will integrate with existing employee data sources (if available)

---

## 7. Constraints

* Limited initial budget -> phased implementation
* Change resistance from employees used to manual processes
* Dependency on HR for accurate initial data migration

---

## 8. Risks & Mitigation

| Risk                                | Impact               | Mitigation                              |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------------  |
| Resistance to new system            | Low adoption         | Conduct training & walkthrough sessions |
| Data inconsistency during migration | Incorrect records    | Perform data validation checks          |
| Delays in approval workflows        | Process inefficiency | Implement automated reminders / SLAs    |
| Lack of stakeholder alignment       | Requirement gaps     | Conduct regular review meetings         |

---

## 9. Success Criteria

* Reduction in manual HR effort
* Faster leave approval turnaround time
* Improved data accuracy and consistency
* Increased employee adoption of HRIS system
* Positive feedback from stakeholders

---

## 10. Dependencies

* Availability of HR policy documentation
* Stakeholder availability for requirement discussions
* Technical readiness for system implementation
* Data readiness for migration

---
